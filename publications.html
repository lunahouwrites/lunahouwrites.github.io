Moon Rabbit

Fractured Lit, April 2025

A better daughter would have counted the signs. The burner under the kettle, left on after brewing tea. The handset laid crooked on its base. But I was small. I had my own asterisms. I saw my mother like I saw the sky: somehow distant, not quite real.

Railroads

One Teen Story, December 2023

My voice hung slack in my throat; it would be many years before I convinced myself I had anything worth saying. All I knew was that the first words I fed to the flames had to be important ones, deliberate ones—the kind of words that surged through your veins and burst from your chest and electrified you with their rightness—and I had not yet found them.

  • familial inheritance, belonging, language, coming-of-age
